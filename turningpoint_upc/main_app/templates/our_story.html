Our Story

In 1971 Rev. Charles L. Killingsworth felt a calling to start a church in Gauiter, Mississippi while living in Cleveland, MS. That same year, he and his family moved to Gautier for the purpose of planting a new church in the community. The first service was held on November 21st 1971 in an old AirForce building purchased from Keesler Air Force Base. The name of the newly founded church was First United Pentecostal Church of Gauiter. From there, in 1977, a new building was built which is still used as the current sanctuary. In 1999, Rev. Charles Killingworth's son, Derrick L. Killingsworth became the pastor and renamed the church to Turning Point UPC, and has faithfully served the role ever since. In 2014, Turning Point built a new Educational Building and Youth Center that currently serves our youth classes. Turning Point has a long and vibrant history, one that cannot be summed up in a simple short paragraph. The amount of lives that have been touched and changed though Turning Point are almost immeasurable. For over 52 years, Turning Point has reached countless lives with the apolostolic message and has seen many souls saved with the outpouring of the Holy Ghost and Jesus name baptism. Turning Point is in a season of revival and continues it's legacy as a Holy Ghost filled church. We welcome all who are spiritually hungry, broken, and seeking something more in their relationship with God. We look forward to worshiping with all.
